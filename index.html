Rotator Cuff Repair: Rehab Phases

Our case is at Week 3, the most vulnerable healing stage.

📍 Our Case

Phase 1 · Weeks 0–6

PROM Only: Tendon Healing

Passive pendulums & ROM. No active motion. Sling use.

Phase 2 · Weeks 6–12

AAROM: Active-Assisted Motion

Pulleys, cane-assisted ROM.

Phase 3 · Weeks 12–16+

AROM & Strengthening

Active motion begins. Resistance bands, isometrics, progressive loading.

Phase 4 · Month 4–6+

Functional Return

Overhead activity, work/sport return, depending on tear size and repair type.

What the Physical Therapist Does

  • 🔍Reviews the Surgeon's OrdersOperative report: tear size, tissue/bone quality, repair technique
  • 📋Writes the Plan of CareTranslates the surgeon's orders into PROM-only precautions
  • 🔄Authorizes All ProgressionClears each phase: PROM → AAROM → AROM, then updates the PTA's instructions
  • ⚖️Retains Full AccountabilityOwns the outcome of Marcus's repair, even on days a PTA runs the session

The PT documents the plan of care that directs every PTA session

Pagliarulo3 (pp. 33, 53).

What the PTA Does

  • 🤜Executes PROM SessionsGuides passive shoulder movement within the PT's plan
  • 👀Actively Monitors the PatientWatches for guarding, shrugging, or requests to advance early
  • 🧠Applies Clinical JudgmentCan adjust hand placement or pendulum arc, not the precaution itself
  • 📞Reports Back, Same SessionAny clinically significant change requires immediate communication4

The PTA guides passive ROM within the PT's plan

"Clinical problem solving includes the ability to adjust, modify, or discontinue an intervention within the plan of care established by the PT based on clinical indications." (Pagliarulo, p. 50)

APTA ethical standards4; Pagliarulo3 (p. 50).

How the PT and PTA Cooperate

  • 📄Shares Surgical DocumentationPT passes along tear size, tissue quality, and repair technique from the operative report
  • 💬Educates the Patient TogetherBoth reinforce the same PROM-only precautions every session, not just at intake
  • 🔄Regular Two-Way CommunicationPTA raises questions, PT updates precautions as healing progresses
  • 📝Regular DocumentationPTA logs daily observations; only the PT can author and sign the formal progress report
